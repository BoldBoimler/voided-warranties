# 2007 Hummer H3

## Why?

Chasing electrical gremlins.  The front turn signals wouldn't work because the fuse was blown.  New fuses would blow immediately upon replacement.

## Overview
My in laws H3 has a leaky sunroof, and water will run down the frame of the car into the BCM causing shorts.  I saw evidence of water damage, so I pulled the module out, apart, and cleaned it as best I could.

## Inside at a Glance
- Single circuit board

## Worth Voiding?
Not really.  There are no user servicable parts.  Ultimately the module did need to be replaced.

## Notes
I ended up cleaning the board with a ton of Q-tips and isopropyl.  I was hoping the corrosion was conductive and causing the shorts.  No evidence this was the case.  There was also thermal damage (some of those traces look toasted).  Ultimately the board had to be replaced.  Replacing it resolved a ton of weird and seemingly unrelated issues (e.g. front turn signal fuse no longer blows when replaced, interrior lights working again, etc.)
